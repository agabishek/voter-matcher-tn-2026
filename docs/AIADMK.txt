Preamble 
The General Election for the 17th Legislative Assembly in Tamil Nadu is 
scheduled to be held on 23.4.2026.  Dedicated entirely to the welfare of  
the people of Tamil Nadu, the 'All India Anna Dravida Munnetra 
Kazhagam'—founded by 'Bharat Ratna' Puratchi Thalaivar M.G.R. and 
sustained by his political heir Puratchi Thalaivi Amma—is facing this 
electoral battlefield with absolute sincerity. 
Perarignar Anna founded the Dravida Munnetra Kazhagam for the 
progress of the Dravidian race, specifically the people of Tamil Nadu.  
He taught us the guiding mantra of 'Duty, Dignity, and Discipline'. 
Furthermore, without deviating from democratic principles, he formed the 
government in Tamil Nadu in 1967 with the overwhelming support  
of the people. 
During his regime, Puratchi Thalaivar Dr. M.G.R. established principles 
like social justice and equality. Moreover, he formulated and implemented 
numerous stellar schemes for the upliftment of the poor and downtrodden. 
However, after Anna's demise, Karunanidhi, who became Chief Minister with 
the support of Puratchi Thalaivar, deliberately hid Perarignar Anna's name. 
Consequently, he kicked away the very ladder that elevated him, exploiting 
the Dravida Munnetra Kazhagam and the government solely to amass 
personal wealth, promote his family's interests, and enrich his heirs. 
To save the people from Karunanidhi's tyrannical rule, 'Ponmana 
Chemmal' Puratchi Thalaivar Dr. M.G.R. founded the Anna Dravida 
Munnetra Kazhagam.  Keeping 'Truth-Hard Work-Upliftment' as his life's 
breath, he successfully led this movement, its cadres, and the people of  
Tamil Nadu. 
He firmly believed that no child should be deprived of education due to 
hunger; hence, he introduced the Nutritious Noon Meal Scheme. 
Furthermore, to ensure that villages and cities received additional facilities and 
that the residents prospered, he introduced the Self-Sufficiency Scheme.

(4) Water Resources

water ponds, and canals, will be restored through the Kudimaramathu 
scheme, and water resources will be augmented. Kudimaramathu works 
will be undertaken jointly with the irrigation farmers' associations of the 
respective areas.

irrigation tanks and supply canals will be completely removed. Similarly, 
steps will be taken to reclaim riverbeds by removing Seemai Karuvelam 
trees that obstruct floodwater flow in the beds of major rivers. 
A high-level committee will be formed regarding the renewal of the 
Parambikulam-Aliyar agreement and the construction of the 
Anaimalaiyar and Nallar dams, which remain unfulfilled in that project, 
and steps will be taken to implement the project. All further steps 
required for the construction of the Anaimalaiyar and Nallar dams will be 
taken and completed swiftly.

Kerala and goes to waste in the sea. Therefore, the Pandiyar
Punnampuzha scheme, a long-time dream of the farmers, will be 
implemented swiftly and comprehensively.
